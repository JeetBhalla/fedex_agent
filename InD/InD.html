Inclusion and Diversity at Accenture

Our commitment to inclusion and diversity begins with our chair and CEO and is sustained by the efforts of all our people to advance a culture of equality, in which everyone feels they belong and can thrive.

How we foster a culture of equality

We take a wide view of diversity and believe no one should be discriminated against regardless of age, disability, race and ethnicity, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation or religion. To help all our people thrive, Accenture offers robust programming, including specialized training, networking support, flexible work arrangements, mentoring, mental health resources, equal benefits to same-sex couples where local law permits and more.

Gender Equality

We believe the future workforce is an equal one, and we’re committed to gender-balance by 2025. Today, 48% of our global workforce are women, and 45% of our board of directors and 26% of our global management committee are women.

Cross Cultural Diversity

Understanding how to work in a cross-cultural environment and leveraging the diversity of multiple cultures helps us be One Accenture. The cultural diversity of our people around the world brings different perspectives to drive innovation and better support our global clients.

Neuro Diversity

Neurodiversity describes natural variations in the human brain and relates to differences in the way individuals think, learn, process and behave. Humans, by nature, are neurodiverse. We're on a journey to create a neuroinclusive working environment that supports neurodivergent individuals to thrive.

Disability Inclusion

We’re creating an accessible, inclusive environment where all our people can belong and thrive. Persons with disabilities at Accenture have access to the latest technology, resources and training for a barrier-free workplace.
